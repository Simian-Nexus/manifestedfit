# The 7-Day Mind-Body Reset

*Voice: Rowan Ellis — calm & minimal. Intended as gated page content, delivered after signup.*

---

Seven days. A few minutes each.

Not a challenge. Not a cleanse. A quiet returning — to your breath, your body, and one small intention at a time.

There is nothing to finish here. Only something to practice.

## How each day works

Each day has four small parts. Together they take five to ten minutes.

**Intention** — one line, set in the morning.
**Breath** — a way to settle.
**Move** — something gentle, never forced.
**Reflect** — one question, answered honestly, at day's end.

If a day feels too big, shrink it. The smallest version still counts.

---

## Day 1 — Arrive

You can't reset a day you haven't noticed yet. Today, you simply arrive.

**Intention:** *I meet today as it is.*

**Breath:** Five slow breaths before your feet touch the floor. Exhale longer than you inhale.

**Move:** Reach both arms overhead. Lengthen. Let them fall. Twice.

**Reflect:** What did I notice today that I usually miss?

## Day 2 — Settle

The body leads. A calm mind follows a calm nervous system, rarely the other way around.

**Intention:** *I let my body feel safe first.*

**Breath:** In for four, out for six. Ten rounds. Nothing else.

**Move:** Roll your shoulders slowly, back to front, five times each way.

**Reflect:** Where did I feel tension leave, even a little?

## Day 3 — Move

Movement is not punishment. Today it is a conversation with a body that has carried you this far.

**Intention:** *I move with kindness, not correction.*

**Breath:** One slow breath before you begin. One after.

**Move:** A soft forward fold. A gentle side bend each way. Hold each for three breaths.

**Reflect:** What did my body ask for that I could give it?

## Day 4 — Nourish

Care is a form of attention. Today you feed the body rather than fix it.

**Intention:** *I give myself what steadies me.*

**Breath:** Before your first drink of the day, pause. Three breaths. Then drink slowly.

**Move:** Stand tall. Rise onto the balls of your feet, lower slowly. Five times.

**Reflect:** What choice today felt like care rather than habit?

## Day 5 — Aim

Manifestation begins here — not in wanting more, but in naming clearly.

**Intention:** *I know one thing I am moving toward.*

**Breath:** Breathe in the feeling of it already being true. Out, release the grip. Five rounds.

**Move:** Place a hand on your chest. Stand still. Feel it rise and fall for one minute.

**Reflect:** If today pointed me one step toward what I want, what was the step?

## Day 6 — Rest

Rest is not the reward for the work. It is the work.

**Intention:** *I let today be enough.*

**Breath:** Lying down tonight, exhale for eight. Repeat until you lose count.

**Move:** Lie on your back. Draw both knees gently toward your chest. Breathe. Release.

**Reflect:** What am I willing to leave unfinished so I can rest?

## Day 7 — Continue

A reset is not a finish line. It is a shape you can return to, any morning, for the rest of your life.

**Intention:** *I keep the smallest version.*

**Breath:** The first breath of Day 1, again. You already know it.

**Move:** Choose the one movement this week that felt best. Do only that.

**Reflect:** Which single ritual will I keep? Begin tomorrow with only that one.
